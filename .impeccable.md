## Design Context

### Users
Tombola Kergo is for school fair organizers, teachers, parent volunteers, and families checking raffle results after an in-person school event. Admin users need a calm, low-friction way to create a tombola, configure prizes, run the draw, and publish results without needing technical knowledge. Public users are often parents or children with a ticket number in hand; they need to quickly understand whether results are available and whether their number won.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel local, simple, and clear. It should carry the warmth of a school fair without becoming childish or visually noisy. The interface should inspire trust in the draw process while keeping a friendly, festive tone suitable for a school community.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light, blueish pastel direction inspired by playful school/classroom products like ClassDojo: soft sky blues, gentle icy pastels, rounded illustration-like surfaces, approachable typography, and clear navigation. The reference direction is playful and welcoming, but Tombola Kergo should remain more restrained and practical than a game world. Avoid dark dashboards, corporate SaaS sterility, harsh neon, heavy purple gradients, and overly character-driven visuals.

### Design Principles
- Make the next action obvious for non-technical school volunteers.
- Keep public result checking fast, readable, and reassuring on mobile.
- Use pastel blue as the primary atmosphere, with small warm accents for celebration and success.
- Favor friendly clarity over dense admin complexity.
- Add festive details sparingly so the raffle feels local and joyful without distracting from legal/admin seriousness.
